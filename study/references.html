Official AAMC resources (the gold standard)

The AAMC writes the MCAT. Their official practice questions and full-length exams are the only ones that reflect true test difficulty and scoring. Prioritize these.

  • What's on the MCAT Exam? — the official content outline (the Foundational Concepts & Content Categories this suite is organized around).
  • Prepare for the MCAT Exam and the AAMC MCAT Official Prep products — Official Guide, Question Packs, Section Banks, and Full-Length Practice Exams 1–4 (the closest thing to the real test).
  • CARS Section Overview — the official description of the three CARS skills (Foundations of Comprehension; Reasoning Within the Text; Reasoning Beyond the Text).
  • How the MCAT Exam Is Scored — the 118–132 per-section and 472–528 total scaled-score system.

In-section citations throughout this suite (e.g. "AAMC Content Category 5A") refer to the official content outline on the "What's on the MCAT Exam?" page linked above.

An honest note on this suite vs. paid prep

The most respected paid third-party question bank is UWorld, known for difficulty and explanation quality; full courses (Kaplan, Princeton Review, Blueprint) bundle content review with qbanks. This suite's questions are original and carefully written, but they are not psychometrically validated against the real exam the way AAMC's are. Treat your percent-correct here as a way to find weak topics and build reps — not as a scaled-score prediction. For score prediction, use the AAMC full-length practice exams.
