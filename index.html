The four sections

★ Highest leverage in Ontario

Critical Analysis & Reasoning (CARS)

No content to memorize — pure reading reasoning. 53 questions / 90 min. The only MCAT section McMaster uses, and a hard cutoff at Western. Most modules + most practice passages live here.

 Section 1

Chemical & Physical Foundations

Gen chem, physics, organic, biochemistry applied to living systems. 59 questions / 95 min. AAMC Foundational Concepts 4 & 5.

 Section 3

Biological & Biochemical Foundations

Biology, biochemistry, with some orgo/gen chem. 59 questions / 95 min. AAMC Foundational Concepts 1–3. Plays to a neuroscience background.

 Section 4

Psychological, Social & Biological Foundations of Behavior

Psychology + sociology + behavioral biology. 59 questions / 95 min. AAMC Foundational Concepts 6–10. Watch the sociology vocabulary — it's the common blind spot for bio/neuro students.

Ontario medical schools — how each uses the MCAT

Ontario schools weight the MCAT very differently. Study strategically: CARS is by far the highest-leverage section across the province.

SchoolMCAT requirementWhat this means for study priority
McMaster (Michael G. DeGroote) Uses only the CARS section in the pre-interview formula (with GPA & CASPer). Other three sections are not scored in admissions. CARS is everything for Mac. Maximize CARS.
Western (Schulich) Hard MCAT cutoffs — a minimum in each section, including a CARS threshold, must be met to be considered. No weak section allowed. CARS cutoff is the classic gatekeeper.
University of Toronto Full MCAT used; competitive total with attention to balance across sections. Balanced high scores; don't neglect any section.
Queen's Full MCAT used as part of the file review / cutoffs. Solid balanced score.
University of Ottawa MCAT not required. Admissions are GPA + CASPer + interview based. Don't over-index here — but a strong MCAT still helps the other schools.
NOSM University MCAT not required. Context/region-focused admissions. Not MCAT-driven.

School-by-school MCAT use confirmed against each school's official admissions pages; always re-verify the current admissions cycle's requirements directly with each program, as cutoffs and formulas are updated yearly.

Strategic takeaway for this applicant Because McMaster is CARS-only and Western gates on a CARS cutoff, CARS is the single highest-return section. This suite intentionally over-weights CARS practice. The good news: a neuroscience background already covers much of the Bio/Biochem and the biological half of Psych/Soc — bank time there and pour it into CARS and the sociology vocabulary.

Scoring reference

Each MCAT section is scaled 118–132 (midpoint 125); the total runs 472–528 (midpoint 500). Ontario competitive applicants typically present totals around 508–515+, with CARS often the limiting section. Percent-correct on these practice sets does not map linearly to the scaled score — use it to track relative improvement and to find weak topics, not to predict an exact scaled score.

Scaled-score ranges per AAMC, "How the MCAT Exam Is Scored." Competitive-range figures are general guidance — confirm current class-profile statistics on each Ontario school's admissions page.
